Rooted in Routine

06 June 2025

My Garden Days I have a plant-care schedule: Mondays are for misting, Thursdays for watering, Sundays for soil checks. This structure stabilizes my week. It’s simple but grounding. The roots I tend in soil mirror the roots I’m growing in life.

Having a schedule gives me rhythm. In a world where days blur together, these small tasks give me milestones. On Sunday, I clean leaves with a damp cloth. On Thursday, I check if the new pot needs drainage. It’s predictable in the best way.

When I feel unproductive or lost, I do something for my plants. I trim, I replant, I rearrange. Even these tiny acts feel like progress. They remind me that care is a kind of productivity, too.

The routine has also taught me to listen more closely—to my plants, to my environment, to myself. A yellowing leaf may mean overwatering. A wilted plant may be root-bound. These signs now feel like a secret language I’ve come to understand.

Rooted in this gentle, predictable care, I’ve found unexpected strength. The act of showing up for my plants has become a practice in showing up for my own life.
