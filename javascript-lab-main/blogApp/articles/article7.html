Sunlight, Soil, and Self-Care

06 June 2025

Plants need light, water, and nutrients. So do I. This reminder helps me take better care of myself. When my fern wilts, I check if I’ve also neglected my own needs. Self-care often starts with plant care.

Tending to plants is more than a hobby; it's a mirror. If a plant is drooping, I ask myself, have I been resting enough? If the leaves are burned, I think, have I overexposed myself to stress? These reflections make me pause and adjust.

There’s also a tactile comfort in the process. Feeling soil, watching roots, seeing new shoots appear—it grounds me. Some evenings, when the light is soft and golden, I just sit with them. That quiet moment becomes an act of nourishment for both of us.

Watering my plants has replaced mindless scrolling. Trimming leaves has replaced anxious fidgeting. It’s not a cure, but it is a support system. Nature has its own gentle way of guiding us back to ourselves.
