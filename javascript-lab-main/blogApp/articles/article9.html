Gardening Alone but Not Lonely

06 June 2025

Caring for plants fills a silence that doesn’t feel empty. I talk to my plants. I name them. They don’t reply, but they respond. A new leaf, a subtle bloom—these are their quiet conversations with me.

Living alone in a hostel can feel isolating. But when I’m surrounded by plants, I feel less alone. They offer companionship without pressure. I share my thoughts while watering, sometimes even frustrations. And in return, they offer stillness.

Some nights I sit in the dark, just watching them. Their silhouettes under moonlight are calming. In those moments, I feel held by something beyond language. My plants don’t solve problems, but they anchor me.

They remind me that being alone doesn’t mean being disconnected. There’s life around me, and it listens.
